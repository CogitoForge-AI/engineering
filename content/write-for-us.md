We want practical writing from engineers who are close to the work at Cogito.

Strong posts usually have a few qualities:

- they explain a real problem, not just the final answer
- they include constraints and tradeoffs
- they show what changed after the work shipped
- they are specific enough that another engineer can learn from them

Good formats include architecture notes, incident learnings, migration stories,
tooling writeups, evaluation notes, and field reports from shipping AI systems.

If you are not sure whether something is worth writing up, a good rule is this:
if another team at Cogito could save time by understanding the path you took,
it is probably a strong candidate.

If you are drafting a first post, start with a short outline:

1. What problem were you trying to solve?
2. Why was it difficult?
3. What options did you consider?
4. What did you choose and why?
5. What should others watch out for next time?
