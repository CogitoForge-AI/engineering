The best postmortems do more than explain an outage. They help the next team
recognize the pattern earlier.

A useful postmortem usually answers three things clearly:

1. What happened, in plain language?
2. Why did our systems and processes allow it?
3. What will meaningfully reduce the chance or impact next time?

Blameless writing matters, but clarity matters just as much. If the reader
cannot see the decision points, the document becomes history instead of a tool.
