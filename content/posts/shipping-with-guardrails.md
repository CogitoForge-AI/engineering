Teams move faster when guardrails are part of the design, not a cleanup task at
the end.

For AI product work, that usually means defining a few boundaries before launch:

- what the model is allowed to do
- what confidence signals we trust
- when the system should ask for help
- what we log so we can learn after release

The practical shift is to treat fallbacks, review paths, and evaluation loops as
product features. That keeps launches calm, measurable, and easier to improve.
